## 0. 中文摘要：PSD3 / PSR 是什么？（仅用于快速理解 PSD3 / PSR）

### 0.1 一句话解释

**PSD3 和 PSR 是欧盟对 PSD2 支付服务监管框架的升级包。PSD3 主要管“谁可以做支付服务、如何拿牌照、如何被监管”；PSR 主要管“支付服务和 Open Banking 应该如何实际运营”。**

### 0.2 PSD3 是什么？

**PSD3 = Third Payment Services Directive，第三支付服务指令。**

它主要关注支付机构和电子货币机构的监管框架，例如：

| 领域 | PSD3 重点 |
|---|---|
| 授权 / 牌照 | 谁可以成为支付机构或电子货币机构 |
| 监管 | 监管机构如何监督支付机构 |
| 自有资金 | 支付机构需要满足哪些资本要求 |
| 客户资金保护 | Safeguarding、隔离账户、担保或保险安排 |
| 治理 | 风险、内控、外包、合规、投诉处理 |
| PI / EMI 整合 | 将电子货币机构纳入更统一的支付机构监管框架 |
| 过渡安排 | 已有 PSD2 PI / EMD2 EMI 如何迁移到 PSD3 |

因为 PSD3 是 **Directive（指令）**，所以它需要欧盟成员国转化为本国法律后具体实施。

### 0.3 PSR 是什么？

**PSR = Payment Services Regulation，支付服务法规。**

它主要关注支付服务的日常运营规则，例如：

| 领域 | PSR 重点 |
|---|---|
| Open Banking | 银行 / ASPSP 如何向 AISP、PISP 开放账户和支付接口 |
| Dedicated Interface | 专用 API、接口可用性、性能、测试环境、错误通知 |
| Consent | 用户授权、Consent Dashboard、撤销、恢复、同步 |
| SCA | 强客户认证、AISP 首次访问、PIS 动态绑定、认证体验一致性 |
| PIS | 单笔付款、未来日期付款、Standing Order、多收款人、状态跟踪 |
| Fraud | 交易监控、冒充欺诈、欺诈信息共享 |
| Liability / Refund | 责任判断、赔付、证据、案件管理 |
| Verification of Payee | 收款人名称和账户标识匹配验证 |
| Transparency | 费用、汇率、ATM、客户通知等透明度要求 |

因为 PSR 是 **Regulation（法规）**，所以它会在欧盟范围内更直接适用，成员国解释空间比 PSD2 更小。

### 0.4 PSD2 vs PSD3 / PSR

| 维度 | PSD2 | PSD3 / PSR |
|---|---|---|
| 法律结构 | 主要是一个 Directive，加上 RTS 和各国实施法 | 拆分为 PSD3 Directive + PSR Regulation |
| Open Banking | 引入 AISP / PISP 访问权 | 更明确地约束 API、Consent、SCA、障碍、性能和证据 |
| API | 要求银行提供访问能力，但质量差异大 | Dedicated Interface 要更可用、可测、可监控、可证明 |
| Consent | 有用户同意要求，但 Dashboard 要求弱 | ASPSP 需要 Consent Dashboard，TPP / ASPSP 要同步状态 |
| SCA | 建立强客户认证框架 | 优化 AIS / PIS 认证流程，强调用户旅程一致性和可访问性 |
| PIS | 很多 PSD2 实现只支持简单单笔付款 | PSR 要求更完整的支付发起能力，如 Standing Order、Future-dated Payment |
| Fraud | 重点是 SCA 和未授权交易 | 强化交易监控、冒充欺诈、VoP、赔付和责任证据 |
| PI / EMI | 支付机构和电子货币机构分开监管 | PSD3 整合 PI 和 EMI 监管框架 |

### 0.5 对 Open Banking 项目的核心影响

对已经做过 PSD2 Open Banking 的团队来说，PSD3 / PSR 不是从零重建，而是一次 **Regulatory Uplift**：

1. 复用已有 PSD2 API Gateway、Consent、SCA、TPP Onboarding、Payment API、Fraud、Monitoring 等资产。
2. 补齐 PSR 对 API 性能、接口可用性、障碍移除、Consent Dashboard、PIS 扩展、VoP、Fraud / Refund 的新要求。
3. 把“技术上线”升级为“可监管、可审计、可运营、可持续演进”的合规体系。

### 0.6 给投标的一句话定位

> **我们将基于现有 PSD2 Open Banking 平台资产，帮助银行以更低重构成本完成 PSD3 / PSR 合规升级，覆盖 ASPSP 接口、TPP 平台、PI / EMI 迁移、Consent、SCA、PIS、VoP、Fraud、Refund 和监管证据。**

## 1. Executive summary

You are preparing an **internal IT bid** against third-party vendors for a broad PSD3 / PSR-related programme. The scope is not one simple Open Banking project. It includes:

1. **Bank / ASPSP implementation** — the bank exposes account and payment access to AISPs and PISPs.

> **Bank-owned delivery + PSD2 asset reuse + selective augmentation + AI-accelerated delivery = lower total cost, faster delivery, stronger control, and lower long-term vendor dependency.**

## 2. Regulatory baseline and source-verified status

### 2.1 EU status as of 5 May 2026

The European Commission published the Financial Data Access and Payments package on **28 June 2023**. The package includes a proposal to amend and modernise PSD2 into **PSD3** and establish a **Payment Services Regulation (PSR)**.

As of the Council documentation dated **17 April 2026**, the PSD3 / PSR texts are described as **final compromise texts with a view to agreement at second reading with the European Parliament**. The Council note states that the package includes a PSD3 Directive and a PSR Regulation and that the final compromise texts aim to address PSD2 evaluation challenges, payment fraud, consumer protection, transparency, cash access and technological change.

The Council and European Parliament had reached a provisional political agreement on **27 November 2025**. The Council press release said the new rules aim to fight payment fraud, improve fee transparency, increase consumer protection and promote innovation.

### 2.2 PSD3 vs PSR legal split

| Instrument | Legal form | Main purpose | Project relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PSD3** | Directive | Authorisation, licensing, supervision, safeguarding, own funds, governance, PI / EMI integration | PI / EMI migration, NCA submission, prudential and governance remediation |
| **PSR** | Regulation | Conduct rules, Open Banking, API access, SCA, fraud, transparency, customer rights, liability | ASPSP API uplift, TPP platform, consent dashboard, PIS expansion, fraud / refund operations |

### 2.3 UK caveat for a major UK bank

For a UK bank, PSD3 / PSR is not automatically the same as a UK mandatory implementation for UK-only business. UK payment services still sit around the UK **Payment Services Regulations 2017**, **Electronic Money Regulations 2011**, FCA rules, open banking requirements and UK regulatory evolution.

However, PSD3 / PSR matters if:

- the banking group has EU entities, EU branches, EU customers or EU-regulated payment services;
- the bank wants group-wide harmonisation of Open Banking / Open Finance capabilities;
- the bank wants to future-proof UK platforms against evolving FCA / UK Open Banking direction;
- the bank is comparing against vendor propositions that may use EU PSD3 / PSR as a transformation driver.

The FCA has indicated that the UK is developing a long-term regulatory framework for Open Banking and Open Finance. In April 2026, the FCA stated that it will consult on its proposed long-term regulatory framework for Open Banking before the end of 2026 and noted that UK open banking has approximately 17 million users, close to one in three UK adults.

### 2.4 Practical regulatory scoping rule

For bid cost control, split scope into three buckets:

| Scope bucket | Meaning | Commercial treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **UK mandatory** | UK PSRs, EMRs, FCA / UK Open Banking obligations | Baseline if in scope |
| **EU mandatory** | PSD3 / PSR obligations for EU entities / services | Baseline only for affected entities |
| **Group future-ready** | PSD3 / PSR-inspired capabilities for UK or global harmonisation | Optional or strategic package |

This prevents both under-scoping and over-scoping.

## 3. What PSD3 / PSR means in simple terms

### 3.1 PSD3 in simple terms

PSD3 is the **licence and supervision rulebook**.

It asks:

- Who is allowed to provide payment services?
- What must a payment institution submit to the regulator?
- What governance, safeguarding and own-funds controls must exist?
- How are e-money institutions integrated into the payment institution framework?
- How do existing PSD2 PI and EMD2 EMI licences transition?

### 3.2 PSR in simple terms

PSR is the **operating rulebook**.

It asks:

- How must banks expose Open Banking access?
- How must AISP / PISP services access accounts and initiate payments?
- How must SCA work?
- How must consent be displayed, managed, withdrawn and synchronised?
- How must payment fraud be monitored?
- How must Verification of Payee work?
- When must customers be refunded?
- What evidence must the institution retain?

### 3.3 Why the EU created both

Under PSD2, many rules were implemented through national legislation and interpreted through RTS, EBA opinions, local regulators and bank-specific operating models. This created differences across countries and banks.

PSD3 / PSR separates:

| Question | Answered mainly by |
|---|---|
| Who can provide services? | PSD3 |
| How must services be provided? | PSR |
| How should payment and e-money institutions be supervised? | PSD3 |
| How should Open Banking APIs, consent, SCA and fraud work? | PSR |

## 4. PSD2 vs PSD3 / PSR deep dive

| Area | PSD2 position | PSD3 / PSR change | IT delivery implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legal structure | One main Directive plus RTS and national implementation | Split into PSD3 Directive + PSR Regulation | Rebuild compliance matrix by role and by instrument |
| Open Banking access | Introduced AISP / PISP rights | More explicit API, consent, SCA, obstacle and performance rules | PSD2 APIs are reusable but need regulatory uplift |
| Dedicated interface | Required secure communication but implementation varied | ASPSPs must provide dedicated interface unless exempted | API gateway, sandbox, documentation, support, monitoring |
| API parity | Often inconsistently achieved | Dedicated interface must provide at least comparable availability / performance and functional parity | Compare customer channel vs TPP channel |
| API performance | Often not regulator-ready | Availability, unplanned downtime and performance statistics become key | API observability and regulatory MI |
| Prohibited obstacles | EBA opinions and supervisory interpretation | Clearer list of prohibited open banking obstacles | Obstacle Compliance Test Pack |
| Consent | Consent existed but dashboard not mature | ASPSP consent dashboard, consent data exchange, withdrawal, re-establishment, history | Consent orchestration layer |
| AIS SCA | Friction and inconsistent renewal rules | First access by AISP, subsequent access generally not re-SCA unless fraud grounds; AISP-side periodic SCA | SCA logic refactor and evidence |
| PIS | Many implementations limited to single payments | Future-dated payments, standing orders, revocation, multi-beneficiary and payment status | PIS expansion programme |
| Verification of Payee | Not fully harmonised under PSD2 | Stronger payee name / account identifier verification before transfer | VoP integration and evidence |
| Fraud | Focus on SCA and unauthorised transactions | Broader monitoring, spoofing / impersonation fraud, refund / liability evidence | Fraud and refund operations layer |
| PI / EMI | Payment institutions and e-money institutions separate | PSD3 aligns frameworks and repeals PSD2 / EMD2 | PI / EMI migration tower |

### 9.3 Fraud / refund case workflow

1. Customer reports fraud, unauthorised transaction or impersonation / spoofing scenario.
2. Case management system captures transaction, SCA, VoP, customer notification and fraud-score evidence.
3. Operations classify the case: unauthorised, authorised push-payment style fraud, impersonation, defective execution, VoP failure, PISP failure, user gross negligence question.
4. Evidence is reviewed against liability rules and internal policy.
5. Refund / refusal decision is approved.
6. Customer communication is issued.
7. Root cause and regulatory MI are updated.
8. Controls are updated if monitoring or journey failed.

## 14. Step 3 — Change-control pool

### 14.1 Why mandatory

A change-control pool is essential because:

1. PSD3 is a Directive and national transposition may create country-level work.
2. PSR is a Regulation, but EBA RTS and supervisory guidance will add operational detail.
3. UK open banking / open finance regulation is evolving separately.
4. Final OJ text may change details.
5. NCAs may interpret evidence and implementation expectations differently.
6. The client may add entities, countries, channels, products or platforms.

### 14.2 Recommended name

Use:

> **Regulatory and Scope Change-Control Pool**

or:

> **PSD3 / PSR Regulatory Adaptation Reserve**

Avoid simply calling it “contingency.”

### 14.3 Chargeable through pool

| Change type | Example |
|---|---|
| Final text delta | Official Journal text differs from baseline |
| EBA RTS / guideline update | Interface reporting or recovery-time details |
| NCA-specific requirement | Extra evidence or local process |
| Country expansion | Additional Member State |
| Entity expansion | Additional PI, EMI, bank entity or branch |
| Product expansion | Cards, wallets, merchant acquiring, corporate payments |
| API standard change | Berlin Group / STET / custom changes |
| VoP provider selection | Provider chosen after design |
| New fraud typology | Additional scenario or workflow |
| Legacy discovery | Hidden dependency not disclosed during Phase 1 |

### 14.4 Not chargeable through pool

| Not chargeable | Reason |
|---|---|
| Defects in agreed deliverables | Delivery responsibility |
| Vendor / delivery misunderstanding | Delivery responsibility |
| Work already included in SOW | Already priced |
| Normal PMO | Base programme cost |
| Missed acceptance criteria | Delivery obligation |

### 14.5 Recommended pool size

| Programme maturity | Suggested pool |
|---|---:|
| Strong platform, single country | 10–12% |
| Multi-country, bank + TPP + PI / EMI | 15–20% |
| Large group, many legacy systems, unclear NCA path | 20–25% |

For this scope, recommend **15–20%**.

## 16. Internal IT vs third-party vendor: cost strategy

### 16.1 Winning argument

Do not say:

> We are cheaper because we use internal people.

Say:

> **We are cheaper because we avoid duplicate discovery, minimise vendor margin on bank-specific integration, reuse existing production platforms, reduce handover and run cost, retain IP, and control future regulatory change internally.**

### 16.2 TCO model

Vendor total bank cost:

```text
Vendor TCO =
Vendor implementation fee
+ vendor licence / platform cost
+ retained bank team cost
+ bank integration team cost
+ security and architecture review cost
+ environment and infrastructure cost
+ data migration / mapping cost
+ regulatory evidence support cost
+ change requests
+ production handover
+ run support
+ future enhancement cost
+ exit / lock-in cost
```

Internal IT total bank cost:

```text
Internal IT TCO =
Incremental internal delivery cost
+ contractor / specialist augmentation where needed
+ existing platform enhancement cost
+ licence / infrastructure delta
+ testing and regulatory evidence cost
+ production support delta
+ controlled change reserve
```

### 16.3 Cost book structure

| Cost area | Internal IT cost driver | Vendor cost challenge |
|---|---|---|
| Regulatory analysis | Internal compliance, legal, product, architecture | Does vendor include bank legal / compliance time? |
| ASPSP API uplift | Existing API gateway, payments and OB stack | Does vendor include core banking / payment integration? |
| TPP platform | Existing connectors, data model, consent, IAM | Are connector exceptions included? |
| Consent dashboard | Existing digital channels and consent store | Is web/mobile integration included? |
| SCA | Existing IAM / MFA / risk auth | Does vendor include all journeys? |
| PIS expansion | Existing payment hub / status services | Are standing orders / future-dated / multi-beneficiary included? |
| VoP / fraud / refund | Existing fraud and case tooling | Is case management included or only API integration? |
| PI / EMI migration | Existing regulatory records and entity knowledge | Does vendor include NCA response and remediation? |
| Testing / evidence | Existing QA and audit governance | Is article-level regulatory evidence included? |
| Production run | Existing service desk and monitoring | Is L2 / L3 support included after go-live? |
| Future change | Internal roadmap ownership | What are vendor CR rates? |

### 26.1 Core positioning

> **As the bank’s internal technology organisation, we propose an internal-led PSD3 / PSR and Open Banking transformation that minimises total bank cost by reusing existing PSD2 / Open Banking, IAM, SCA, payments, fraud, digital-channel, monitoring and operational platforms. Unlike a third-party-led model, our approach avoids duplicate discovery, reduces integration margin, preserves internal IP, lowers service-transition cost and gives the bank direct control over future UK, EU and supervisory change.**

### 26.2 Four-step delivery wording

> **Given the breadth of scope across ASPSP, TPP / AISP / PISP and PI / EMI roles, we recommend a controlled four-step delivery model. First, a fixed-price PSD3 / PSR Blueprint phase will establish the regulatory obligation matrix, current-state assessment, target architecture, implementation packages and evidence model. Second, implementation will be delivered through modular packages for ASPSP dedicated interfaces, consent dashboard, AISP / PISP platform, PI / EMI migration, fraud / VoP / refund operations and regulatory evidence. Third, a dedicated regulatory change-control pool will manage final text, EBA RTS, FCA / NCA and country-specific changes. Fourth, reusable accelerators will reduce delivery risk and shorten time to compliance.**

### 26.3 AI delivery wording

> **The baseline delivery model will use controlled AI-assisted delivery to reduce analysis, documentation, testing, evidence and PMO effort while retaining human accountability for regulatory interpretation, architecture decisions, security approvals and production release. The enhanced option will establish an agentic AI delivery factory for high-volume, evidence-heavy workstreams, using specialised agents to generate regulatory traceability, requirements, API specifications, test packs, code-change drafts, change-impact assessments and audit evidence with mandatory human approval gates.**

### 26.4 Cost comparison wording

> **A third-party vendor may offer a lower visible implementation fee, but internal IT offers the lower total bank cost. The bank will still need retained internal teams, security review, integration support, release governance, operations handover, regulatory evidence, change management and future enhancements under a vendor-led model. Our internal-led model avoids duplicate discovery, minimises vendor margin on bank-specific integration, retains IP, reduces run cost and preserves long-term change capability.**
