I’m reading your last phrase as: **you already built PSD2 open banking, and you now want to bid a PSD3/PSR migration or implementation project.**

## 1. Executive view

The EU payment-services reform is **not just “PSD2 upgraded to PSD3.”** It is a new two-layer regime:

**PSD3** is mainly the Directive for **authorisation, licensing, supervision, prudential rules, payment institutions, and e-money integration**. **PSR** is the directly applicable Regulation for **conduct rules, open banking, payment execution, SCA, fraud, liability, transparency, and user rights**. The Council final-compromise note describes the package as a Directive and a Regulation, and the PSD3 text says the EU is separating conduct rules into a Regulation from authorisation/supervision rules in the Directive. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8220-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

For your existing PSD2 open-banking solution, the biggest bid-impact is this: **the PSD2 API stack is reusable, but PSD3/PSR turns many previously interpreted PSD2/RTS practices into more explicit legal obligations.** The project is likely less about building “open banking from zero” and more about proving API quality, removing obstacles, adding consent dashboards, expanding PIS capabilities, strengthening fraud controls, improving SCA journeys, and creating regulatory evidence packs.

As of the current Council final compromise text, the package is still best treated as **final compromise text with a view to second-reading agreement**, not as the final Official Journal version. Your bid should include a change-control item for final OJ text and later EBA RTS/guidelines. The Council note says the texts are proposed final compromise texts and recommends COREPER approval with a view to agreement at second reading. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8220-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

---

## 2. Why PSD3/PSR exists

PSD2 achieved important things: it introduced regulated **payment initiation services**, **account information services**, **third-party provider access**, and strong customer authentication/open communication standards. The EBA says PSD2 applies since 2018 and requires ASPSPs to establish access interfaces through which TPPs can access customer payment accounts securely. ([European Banking Authority](https://www.eba.europa.eu/publications-and-media/press-releases/eba-publishes-opinion-obstacles-provision-third-party))

But PSD2 had pain points: inconsistent national implementation, divergent interpretations, API obstacles, SCA friction, fraud, consumer-protection gaps, and uneven treatment of payment institutions/e-money institutions. The Council final note says the new PSD3/PSR texts aim to address PSD2 evaluation challenges, tackle payment fraud, increase consumer protection, boost transparency, improve access to cash, and adapt rules to market and technology changes. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8220-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

## 3. PSD2 vs PSD3/PSR: board-level comparison

| Area | PSD2 position | PSD3/PSR change | Bid impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legal structure | PSD2 was a Directive, with conduct, authorisation, transparency, open banking, SCA and rights spread across PSD2 plus RTS and national implementation. | PSD3/PSR splits the regime: conduct rules move into PSR; authorisation/supervision of payment institutions sit in PSD3. PSD3 also replaces PSD2 and EMD2. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8222-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Your compliance mapping should be rebuilt by **PSR article obligation**, not only by PSD2/RTS controls. |
| Open banking access right | PSD2 gave AIS/PIS rights, but API interpretation and enforcement varied. | PSR explicitly says PSPs must not prevent users from using PISPs/AISPs for online-accessible payment accounts. It also confirms no contract is required between AISP/PISP and ASPSP, and access must remain possible even where multilateral arrangements exist. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Bid must include an **open-banking legal-obstacle review** and API journey remediation. |
| Dedicated interface | PSD2/RTS allowed dedicated interfaces and addressed obstacles, but market implementation differed. | PSR requires ASPSPs with online-accessible payment accounts to have at least one dedicated interface for AIS/PIS data exchange. Documentation must be available, standards must be recognised, changes require advance notice, and testing facilities must be provided. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Existing PSD2 API gateway can be reused, but must be upgraded for **documentation, test facility, change notice, error messaging, performance statistics, and evidence**. |
| API performance and availability | PSD2 RTS had interface requirements, but banks/TPPs often disagreed on quality and outages. | PSR requires quarterly publication of dedicated-interface availability, unplanned unavailability and performance, compared with customer-facing interfaces. It also requires TPP testing facilities and error notifications explaining unexpected events. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Add **API observability, SLA dashboards, TPP success-rate monitoring, outage classification, and quarterly reporting**. |
| Prohibited obstacles | PSD2 obstacles were interpreted through RTS and EBA opinions. EBA previously identified issues such as mandatory redirection, multiple SCAs, manual IBAN entry, and additional consent checks. ([European Banking Authority](https://www.eba.europa.eu/publications-and-media/press-releases/eba-publishes-opinion-obstacles-provision-third-party)) | PSR codifies a long list of prohibited obstacles: manual unique identifier entry in ASPSP domain, ASPSP checks of user permission to TPP, extra TPP registrations, restricting PIS payees to beneficiary lists, domestic-only identifiers, more SCA than direct channels, unsupported authentication methods, and redirection/decoupled journeys with extra steps. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Create an **obstacle test suite**: redirection parity, decoupled journey parity, SCA count, beneficiary-list rules, identifier coverage, and TPP onboarding checks. |
| Consent management | PSD2 required consent, but did not impose the same detailed ASPSP dashboard model. | PSR requires ASPSPs to provide a user dashboard for AIS/PIS consents, including provider name, account, purpose, validity, data categories and access dates. Users must be able to withdraw access free of charge, and ASPSP/TPP cooperation is required to keep dashboard data current. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | This is likely a **new build** unless your PSD2 platform already has bank-side consent dashboards and ASPSP/TPP consent event synchronisation. |
| AIS SCA model | PSD2 SCA rules and exemptions created friction. The EBA noted PSD2 RTS previously required first access and at least every 90 days for account-information access, and later moved toward 180-day renewal for account information. ([European Banking Authority](https://www.eba.europa.eu/sites/default/files/document_library/Publications/Draft%20Technical%20Standards/2022/EBA-RTS-2022-03%20RTS%20on%20SCA%26CSC/1029858/Final%20Report%20on%20the%20amendment%20of%20the%20RTS%20on%20SCA%26CSC.pdf)) | PSR says ASPSPs apply SCA for the first access by a given AISP, but not for later access unless there are reasonable fraud grounds; AISPs must apply SCA when the user accesses account information through the AISP at least 180 days after last SCA, using either their own or ASPSP SCA. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Refactor AIS journeys, consent renewal, background access, risk triggers, and AISP-side SCA capability. |
| PIS functionality | Many PSD2 implementations supported basic single payment initiation only, depending on bank and API standard. | PSR requires dedicated interfaces to support, at minimum, standing order placement/revocation, single payments, future-dated payments, multiple beneficiaries, and payments to payees not on the beneficiary list unless the user cannot do this directly in the customer interface. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Expand PIS APIs and test cases beyond “single immediate payment.” |
| Verification of payee | PSD2 did not create a broad, PSR-level payee-name/identifier framework. | PSR extends verification of payee concepts to credit transfers, including those outside Regulation 260/2012 scope, and recitals describe near-real-time notification before authorisation where payee name and unique identifier do not match. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Include **VoP integration**, mismatch UX, liability workflow, and PISP/ASPSP responsibility mapping. |
| Fraud and liability | PSD2 focused heavily on SCA and unauthorised-payment liability. | PSR adds stronger fraud prevention: payer and payee PSP transaction monitoring, refund consequences when monitoring duties fail, impersonation-fraud liability, and cross-sector fraud cooperation. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Add fraud-engine integration, case management, evidence capture, refund decisioning, police-report handling, and payee-bank cooperation. |
| Spending limits and blocking | PSD2 allowed certain payment-instrument limits/blocking. | PSR requires PSPs to offer user-set maximum transfer limits by payment means/instrument, per transaction or timeframe; remote limit increases may have a four-hour delay, and PSPs must block or refuse execution in defined circumstances. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Add customer-configurable limits, cooling-off delay, notification, and payment-order refusal logic. |
| E-money | PSD2 and EMD2 were separate regimes. | PSD3 repeals both PSD2 and EMD2 and aligns payment institution and e-money institution supervision, while preserving some e-money specificities. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8222-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | For PI/EMI clients, include licensing, safeguarding, capital, register and transition analysis. |
| Transition | PSD2-authorised entities continue under transition rules. | PSD3 allows PSD2-authorised payment institutions to continue without new authorisation until 27 months after PSD3 entry into force, provided required information is submitted and competent authorities assess compliance. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8222-2026-INIT/en/pdf)) | Bid should include a **regulatory transition workstream** for authorisation evidence and NCA submission packs. |

## 4. Deep dive: what changes for your open-banking platform

### 4.1 ASPSP dedicated interface compliance

For a bank or ASPSP implementation, PSD3/PSR moves the open-banking API from “PSD2 API exists” to “PSD3/PSR-grade regulated interface.” The dedicated interface must support secure data exchange with AISPs and PISPs, use recognised standards, expose documentation, publish a public summary, provide change notifications at least two months before non-emergency changes, and maintain testing facilities without sharing sensitive payment data or personal data. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

**Bid work packages:**

1. **API compliance assessment** against PSR Article 35-48 obligations.  
2. **Interface documentation factory**: endpoint catalogue, schemas, error codes, auth flows, throttling rules, release notes.  
3. **Developer/testing portal** for authorised TPPs and applicants.  
4. **Quarterly API reporting**: availability, unplanned downtime, response time, AIS success rate, PIS success rate, PIS transaction volume success rate.  
5. **Operational runbook**: planned outage one-month notice, emergency-change evidence, incident notifications, and NCA evidence production.

### 4.2 API parity and functional scope

The PSR dedicated interface must not be worse than the ASPSP customer interface. Response time for AISP/PISP access requests must not be longer than the direct customer interface. AIS data must include account identifier, account holder name, currencies, balance, and pending instrument transactions where visible in the customer interface. PIS must support standing orders, single payments, future-dated payments, multiple beneficiaries, and payments to non-beneficiary-list payees where the customer interface permits them. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

**Key gap against many PSD2 implementations:**  
Most PSD2 APIs were designed around account lists, balances, transactions, and basic single payment initiation. PSD3/PSR requires deeper **channel parity**. The API product backlog should include:

| Capability | PSD3/PSR target state |
|---|---|
| Account data | Account identifier, account holder name, currency, balances, transactions, visible pending payments/card transactions |
| Payment initiation | Single, standing order, revoke standing order, future-dated, revoke future-dated, multiple beneficiaries |
| Payment status | Immediate initiation status and ongoing execution status until executed or rejected |
| Authentication | All customer-facing authentication methods must be supported for TPP journeys unless properly justified |
| Performance | No worse than direct customer channel |
| Errors | Explain unexpected events and errors to TPPs |

### 4.3 Prohibited obstacles: the area most likely to fail audits

The PSR’s explicit list of prohibited obstacles is one of the biggest changes for PSD2-built systems. Under PSD2, many “obstacle” disputes were handled through EBA guidance and supervisory interpretation. Under PSR, many practices become directly listed. This includes manual identifier entry in the ASPSP domain, ASPSP checks of customer permission to the TPP, extra registrations, restricting PIS to existing beneficiary lists, domestic-only identifiers, more SCAs than direct journeys, unsupported authentication methods, and redirection or decoupled journeys that add steps compared with the direct channel. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

**Recommended bid deliverable:** an **Obstacle Compliance Test Pack**, with automated and manual tests covering:

| Test area | Example evidence |
|---|---|
| SCA count parity | PIS journey via TPP has no more SCA steps than equivalent direct channel |
| Redirection parity | Redirect/decoupled journey has no additional required actions |
| Authentication coverage | Every direct-channel authentication method is supported or exception-documented |
| Identifier coverage | Domestic-only restrictions removed where not justified |
| Beneficiary-list parity | PIS can initiate to non-saved payees where direct channel allows |
| Consent-check removal | ASPSP does not re-check user permission given to TPP beyond permitted dashboard data exchange |
| TPP onboarding | No unnecessary extra registration beyond security/contact exchange needed for the dashboard and operations |

### 4.4 Consent dashboard and consent-event synchronisation

This is a major product change. PSR requires ASPSPs to provide a dashboard, integrated into the user interface, where users can monitor and manage ongoing AIS/PIS consents. The dashboard must show provider name, account, purpose, validity, data categories, and dates of account data access. Users must be able to withdraw access free of charge, re-establish withdrawn access within 48 hours, and see records of expired/withdrawn consents for two years. The dashboard must be easy to find, neutral, clear, and must not use discouraging language or manipulative design. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

The ASPSP and AISP/PISP must also cooperate so dashboard information is kept current. TPPs must provide consent information to the ASPSP, and ASPSPs must notify TPPs of dashboard changes, including withdrawal. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

**Bid architecture implication:**  
You need a **Consent Orchestration Layer**, not just OAuth consent screens. It should support:

| Component | Function |
|---|---|
| Consent registry | Stores consent ID, TPP, account, purpose, validity, data categories, status |
| Consent dashboard UI | Customer-facing web/mobile view with neutral wording and withdrawal |
| Consent event API | TPP → ASPSP new consent events; ASPSP → TPP withdrawal/change events |
| Data deletion workflow | TPP ceases access/use and deletes data after withdrawal subject to PSR timing and customer choices |
| Audit | Two-year withdrawn/expired consent record; access-date evidence |

### 4.5 AIS and SCA change

PSD2 created substantial AIS friction because ASPSPs had different approaches to SCA exemptions and re-authentication. The EBA noted that PSD2 SCA exemptions for account information were historically tied to first access and at least every 90 days, and that divergent practices created friction. ([European Banking Authority](https://www.eba.europa.eu/sites/default/files/document_library/Publications/Draft%20Technical%20Standards/2022/EBA-RTS-2022-03%20RTS%20on%20SCA%26CSC/1029858/Final%20Report%20on%20the%20amendment%20of%20the%20RTS%20on%20SCA%26CSC.pdf))

PSR moves to a clearer split: the ASPSP applies SCA for the first access to payment account data by a given AISP, but not subsequent access unless there are reasonable fraud grounds; the AISP must apply SCA when the user accesses account information through it at least 180 days after SCA was last applied, using either its own SCA or the ASPSP’s. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

**Bid impact:**

For an **ASPSP**: refactor risk triggers so SCA is not re-imposed on AISP access unless reasonable fraud grounds exist.  
For an **AISP**: implement or integrate AISP-side SCA for 180-day renewal.  
For both: align consent validity, SCA timestamp, fraud-trigger evidence, and dashboard status.

### 4.6 PIS expansion

Many PSD2 open-banking projects focused on AIS first and simple PIS later. PSD3/PSR will make PIS more demanding. Dedicated interfaces must allow PISPs to place and revoke standing orders, initiate single payments, initiate and revoke future-dated payments, initiate payments to multiple beneficiaries, and initiate payments to payees outside the beneficiary list where the direct customer interface allows that. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

**Bid backlog:**

| PIS feature | Build item |
|---|---|
| Standing order | Create, amend where applicable, revoke, status |
| Future-dated payment | Create, revoke, status until execution/rejection |
| Multiple beneficiaries | Batch/multi-beneficiary model, validation, SCA dynamic linking |
| Non-saved payee | Beneficiary-list bypass where direct channel supports it |
| Status updates | Immediate and ongoing status from initiation to execution/rejection |
| Error handling | Rejection reason, insufficient funds, VoP mismatch, limit exceeded, fraud suspension |

### 4.7 Verification of payee

PSR aligns with the verification-of-payee framework and applies name/identifier mismatch rules to credit transfers, including those outside Regulation 260/2012 scope. Recitals describe a service that checks whether the payee unique identifier and payee name match, notifies the payer of mismatch within a few seconds, and does so before the payer authorises the transaction. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

**Bid work packages:**

| Workstream | Deliverables |
|---|---|
| VoP service integration | Scheme/provider integration, matching API, response mapping |
| UX | Match, close match, no match, unavailable, override handling |
| PISP flow | PISP-initiated payment VoP orchestration |
| Liability evidence | Store request/response, customer warning, customer decision |
| Corporate support | Legal-person identifiers such as fiscal number, EUID or LEI where applicable |

### 4.8 Fraud monitoring, impersonation fraud, and refund operations

PSD3/PSR is materially more fraud-focused than PSD2. PSR recitals say both payer and payee PSPs should have transaction monitoring, going beyond SCA. Payer PSP monitoring happens before execution; payee PSP monitoring happens before funds are made available. Where monitoring obligations fail and the transaction is fraud-related, the payer should be refunded, and the payee PSP may have to reimburse the payer PSP if the payee PSP failed. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

PSR also introduces impersonation-fraud liability: where a consumer is manipulated by a third party pretending to be the consumer’s PSP using PSP-attributed communication channels, and this causes fraudulent authorised transactions, the PSP must refund the full amount if the consumer notifies the PSP and reports the fraud to police, unless the consumer acted fraudulently or with gross negligence. The PSP has the burden of proving fraud or gross negligence. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

**Bid architecture implication:**  
Add a **Fraud and Liability Operations Layer**:

| Module | Function |
|---|---|
| Real-time transaction monitoring | Behavioural, device, session, account, payee and transaction-risk scoring |
| PIS risk scoring | Include PISP-originated transactions in monitoring |
| Payee-risk monitoring | Payee PSP checks before funds availability |
| Fraud suspension | Hold/refuse/suspend flows with reason codes and customer contact |
| Refund case management | 15-business-day investigation/refund/refusal path for impersonation fraud |
| Evidence store | SCA evidence, VoP evidence, fraud score, customer warning, customer contact, police report, decision rationale |
| Cross-sector reporting | Hosting/online platform/communications provider reporting where relevant |

### 4.9 Spending limits, blocking, and mobile app activation

PSR requires PSPs to offer payment users maximum transfer limits that may differ by payment means or instrument, on a per-transaction or timeframe basis. Remote increases may be subject to a four-hour delay, and PSPs must refuse execution where an order exceeds the maximum amount. PSPs may also block instruments for security, suspected unauthorised/fraudulent use, or credit-risk reasons, and must assess whether blocking remains justified within two business days. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

For bids, this is a cross-channel product change: **direct channel, PIS channel, mobile app, customer-service tooling, notifications, and fraud tooling must all use the same limit/block state.**

# PSD3 / PSR 深度分析：与 PSD2 对比及 Open Banking 项目投标建议

## 1. 核心结论

欧盟这次并不是简单把 **PSD2 升级成 PSD3**。更准确地说，新的支付服务监管框架分成两层：

1. **PSD3：Directive，指令**
   主要管：
   - 支付机构授权；
   - 支付机构监管；
   - 资本、保障资金、治理要求；
   - 支付机构与电子货币机构的整合；
   - 各成员国如何转化为本国法律。

2. **PSR：Regulation，法规**
   主要管：
   - Open Banking；
   - 支付执行规则；
   - SCA 强客户认证；
   - 用户权利；
   - 透明度；
   - 欺诈防控；
   - 责任与赔付；
   - ASPSP、AISP、PISP 的具体行为义务。

所以，对你们这种已经做过 **PSD2 Open Banking** 的团队来说，真正影响最大的不是 PSD3 本身，而是 **PSR 里面对 Open Banking、API、SCA、Consent、Fraud、Liability 的细化要求**。

一句话总结：

> **PSD2 Open Banking 的技术底座可以复用，但 PSD3 / PSR 会把很多过去依赖解释、RTS、EBA Opinion 或监管口径的要求，变成更明确、更可审计、更可测试的法律义务。**

所以你们投标时不要把它包装成“从零建设 Open Banking”，而应该定位为：

> **基于现有 PSD2 Open Banking 能力，进行 PSD3 / PSR 合规升级、接口增强、授权管理升级、SCA 流程优化、欺诈与赔付机制补强，并交付监管证据包。**

# 2. PSD2 与 PSD3 / PSR 的核心差异

## 2.1 法规结构变化

| 维度 | PSD2 | PSD3 / PSR |
|---|---|---|
| 法律形式 | 一个 Directive，加上 RTS、Guidelines、各国实施法 | PSD3 是 Directive；PSR 是 Regulation |
| 生效方式 | 需要成员国转化为本国法律 | PSR 直接适用；PSD3 仍需成员国转化 |
| Open Banking 规则 | 在 PSD2 及 RTS 中规定，解释空间较大 | 主要进入 PSR，规则更直接、更统一 |
| 监管重点 | 建立 AISP / PISP 权利、SCA、账户访问接口 | 强化 API 质量、反障碍、Consent Dashboard、欺诈责任、用户保护 |
| 对项目影响 | 建设 Open Banking 基础能力 | 升级为可监管、可审计、可度量的 Open Banking 合规体系 |

# 3. 对 Open Banking 的最大影响

## 3.1 PSD2 做到了什么？

PSD2 的核心贡献是打开银行账户访问能力，引入三类关键角色：

| 角色 | 中文解释 | 典型能力 |
|---|---|---|
| ASPSP | Account Servicing Payment Service Provider，通常是银行或账户服务机构 | 提供账户、余额、交易、支付接口 |
| AISP | Account Information Service Provider，账户信息服务商 | 聚合账户、余额、交易数据 |
| PISP | Payment Initiation Service Provider，支付发起服务商 | 帮用户从银行账户发起付款 |
| TPP | Third Party Provider，第三方服务商 | AISP / PISP 的统称 |

PSD2 要求银行不能无理由阻止合法 TPP 访问用户授权的账户数据，也不能阻止用户通过 PISP 发起支付。

但是 PSD2 在实际落地中出现了很多问题，例如：

- 各银行 API 标准不统一；
- 各国监管解释不一致；
- 有些银行接口可用性差；
- Redirect / Decoupled SCA 流程体验差；
- TPP 调用失败率高；
- 有些银行通过额外步骤形成事实上的“obstacle”；
- Consent 生命周期管理不透明；
- PIS 功能经常只支持最简单的单笔付款；
- 欺诈、责任、赔付边界不够清晰。

PSD3 / PSR 正是在这些痛点上继续强化。

# 4. PSD2 vs PSD3 / PSR 详细对比

| 领域 | PSD2 状态 | PSD3 / PSR 新要求 | 对你们投标的影响 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 法律架构 | PSD2 一个 Directive 覆盖很多内容 | 拆分为 PSD3 + PSR；PSR 直接适用 | 合规矩阵需要从 PSD2 Article / RTS 映射到 PSD3 / PSR Article |
| Open Banking 访问权 | AISP / PISP 有访问权，但落地差异大 | 明确 ASPSP 不得阻止用户使用 AISP / PISP | 需要做 Open Banking 阻碍性设计审查 |
| Dedicated Interface | PSD2 要求提供接口，但很多实现质量不一 | ASPSP 必须提供专用接口，并满足性能、文档、测试、变更通知要求 | 需要 API 升级、文档、Sandbox、监控、报告能力 |
| API 可用性 | PSD2 有要求，但监管证据不足 | 需要发布接口可用性、不可用时间、性能等统计 | 需要 API Observability 和季度报告 |
| Obstacles | 主要通过 EBA Opinion 和监管解释判断 | PSR 更明确列出禁止行为 | 需要障碍测试包和流程整改 |
| Consent 管理 | PSD2 有用户同意要求，但 Dashboard 要求不强 | ASPSP 需要提供 Consent Dashboard | 很可能是新增建设范围 |
| AIS SCA | 过去常见 90 天或 180 天再认证规则，实践不统一 | 首次 AISP 访问由 ASPSP 做 SCA；后续访问一般不应重复要求，除非有合理欺诈风险 | 需要重构 AIS SCA 和 Consent Renewal 逻辑 |
| PIS 功能 | 很多 PSD2 实现只支持单笔立即支付 | PSR 要求支持 Standing Order、Future-dated Payment、Multiple Beneficiaries 等 | PIS API 需要显著扩展 |
| Verification of Payee | PSD2 没有形成统一的强制性机制 | PSR 强化收款人名称与账户标识匹配验证 | 需要接入 VoP，调整 UX 与责任证据 |
| 欺诈与赔付 | 重点是 SCA 与未授权交易责任 | 加强交易监控、冒充欺诈、退款责任、跨行业协作 | 需要 Fraud Engine、Case Management、Evidence Store |
| 用户限额与阻断 | PSD2 已有部分工具 | PSR 要求更明确的用户限额、阻断、通知和恢复机制 | 需要 Limit Management、Blocking Workflow |
| 电子货币机构 | PSD2 与 EMD2 分开 | PSD3 整合支付机构与电子货币机构监管 | 如果客户是 PI / EMI，需要授权迁移和许可评估 |

# 5. Open Banking 技术与合规深度影响

## 5.1 Dedicated Interface：专用接口升级

PSD2 时，很多银行认为“我有 API 就可以”。但 PSD3 / PSR 下，监管重点会变成：

- API 是否稳定；
- API 是否与客户自有渠道能力等价；
- TPP 是否能顺利测试；
- 接口变更是否提前通知；
- 错误信息是否清晰；
- 是否存在人为阻碍；
- 是否能生成监管证据；
- 是否能证明 TPP 体验不低于银行自有渠道。

投标时建议设置一个独立工作流：

## Workstream：Open Banking API Remediation

| 交付物 | 内容 |
|---|---|
| API 合规差距分析 | 对照 PSD3 / PSR 要求检查现有 PSD2 API |
| API 文档升级 | Endpoint、Schema、认证流程、错误码、版本管理 |
| Sandbox / Testing Facility | 面向 TPP 的测试环境和测试数据 |
| API 性能监控 | Availability、Response Time、Success Rate |
| 季度报告 | 可用性、不可用时间、性能、PIS/AIS 成功率 |
| 变更通知机制 | API 变更提前通知、紧急变更留痕 |
| Incident Runbook | 接口故障、TPP 通知、监管证据保留 |

---

## 5.2 API Parity：接口能力必须接近客户自有渠道

PSD3 / PSR 的一个核心方向是：

> TPP 通过 Open Banking API 能做的事情，不应明显弱于用户直接登录银行 App / 网银能做的事情。

这意味着你们需要比较：

| 银行自有渠道能力 | TPP API 是否支持 |
|---|---|
| 查看账户列表 | 是 / 否 |
| 查看账户持有人姓名 | 是 / 否 |
| 查看余额 | 是 / 否 |
| 查看交易明细 | 是 / 否 |
| 查看 Pending Transactions | 是 / 否 |
| 发起单笔付款 | 是 / 否 |
| 发起未来日期付款 | 是 / 否 |
| 创建 Standing Order | 是 / 否 |
| 撤销 Standing Order | 是 / 否 |
| 多收款人付款 | 是 / 否 |
| 向非已保存收款人付款 | 是 / 否 |
| 查询支付状态 | 是 / 否 |
| 使用全部认证方式 | 是 / 否 |

很多 PSD2 项目最大的问题是：**AIS 做得还可以，PIS 做得比较浅。**

PSD3 / PSR 下，PIS 很可能成为差距最大的模块。

---

## 5.3 Obstacles：禁止人为阻碍 TPP

PSD2 时代，一个非常大的争议点是银行是否通过流程设计“合法地制造困难”。例如：

- 要用户手动重新输入 IBAN；
- TPP 路径比银行 App 路径多做一次 SCA；
- Redirect 页面设计复杂；
- 只允许付给已保存收款人；
- 要求 TPP 做额外注册；
- 对国内账户和跨境账户采用不同限制；
- 认证方式不支持 App-to-App；
- TPP 调用接口经常失败但银行自有 App 正常。

PSD3 / PSR 会把这些问题更明确地纳入监管范围。

你们投标时应该提出一个很有价值的交付物：

## Obstacle Compliance Test Pack

| 测试领域 | 测试目标 |
|---|---|
| SCA 步骤数量 | TPP 支付路径不能比银行自有渠道多不必要步骤 |
| Redirect 体验 | Redirect / Decoupled 流程不能额外增加摩擦 |
| 认证方式覆盖 | 用户在银行 App 可用的认证方式，TPP 路径也应支持 |
| 收款人限制 | 不能无理由只允许已保存收款人 |
| 账户标识限制 | 不能无理由只支持本地账户标识 |
| Consent 检查 | ASPSP 不应重新审查用户给 TPP 的授权 |
| TPP 接入流程 | 不应要求无必要的额外注册或人工流程 |
| Error Handling | 失败原因应清晰、可解释、可排查 |

这部分非常适合做成你们投标的差异化卖点。

# 6. Consent Dashboard：PSD3 / PSR 下的重要新增能力

PSD2 下，Consent 通常存在于：

- TPP 端；
- OAuth 授权流程；
- 银行端简单授权记录；
- API Consent Object。

但 PSD3 / PSR 要求更强的用户可视化和可管理能力。

ASPSP 需要向用户提供一个 **Consent Dashboard**，让用户可以看到：

| Dashboard 字段 | 说明 |
|---|---|
| TPP 名称 | 哪个 AISP / PISP 获得授权 |
| 授权账户 | 哪些账户被授权访问 |
| 授权目的 | 数据访问或支付发起目的 |
| 有效期 | 授权起止时间 |
| 数据类别 | 账户、余额、交易等数据类型 |
| 访问日期 | TPP 实际访问账户数据的日期 |
| 授权状态 | Active / Withdrawn / Expired |
| 撤销入口 | 用户可以免费撤销授权 |

关键点是：

> 这不只是一个页面，而是一个 Consent 生命周期管理系统。

你们需要设计：

## Consent Orchestration Layer

| 模块 | 功能 |
|---|---|
| Consent Registry | 记录 Consent ID、TPP、账户、目的、数据范围、有效期 |
| Customer Dashboard | 用户查看、撤销、恢复授权 |
| TPP Event API | TPP 向 ASPSP 推送授权信息 |
| ASPSP Event API | ASPSP 向 TPP 通知授权撤销或变更 |
| Access Log | 记录 TPP 实际访问日期 |
| Data Deletion Workflow | 授权撤销后的数据停止使用和删除流程 |
| Audit Evidence | 保留撤销、过期、访问和恢复记录 |

这个模块非常适合作为你们从 PSD2 升级到 PSD3 / PSR 的重点报价项。

---

# 7. SCA：强客户认证的变化

PSD2 已经引入 SCA，但实际体验经常很差，尤其是在 AIS 场景：

- 用户需要反复认证；
- 90 天 / 180 天规则解释不一致；
- 银行端频繁触发 SCA；
- TPP 体验不稳定；
- App-to-App、Redirect、Decoupled 流程差异大。

PSD3 / PSR 的方向是：

1. **首次 AISP 访问账户数据时，ASPSP 应执行 SCA；**
2. **后续 AISP 访问一般不应再次强制 SCA，除非有合理欺诈风险；**
3. **AISP 需要在用户通过 AISP 查看账户信息时，按周期执行 SCA；**
4. **SCA 方式应更具包容性，不能只依赖智能手机；**
5. **TPP 路径不能比银行自有渠道有更多不必要认证步骤。**

你们项目中应重点检查：

| 检查项 | 问题 |
|---|---|
| AIS 首次访问 | 是否正确触发 ASPSP SCA |
| AIS 后续访问 | 是否无理由重复触发 SCA |
| 180 天周期 | 是否记录 SCA 时间戳 |
| AISP-side SCA | AISP 是否具备自己的 SCA 能力 |
| App-to-App | 是否支持移动端顺滑认证 |
| Redirect | 是否比自有渠道多步骤 |
| Accessibility | 是否支持非智能手机用户 |
| Fraud Trigger | 是否有“合理欺诈风险”证据 |

---

# 8. PIS：支付发起能力需要扩展

很多 PSD2 Open Banking 项目中，PIS 只做到：

- 单笔立即付款；
- 查询付款状态；
- 简单 SCA；
- 简单 Reject / Success 状态。

但 PSD3 / PSR 下，PIS 范围更广，至少要关注：

| PIS 能力 | PSD3 / PSR 下的目标能力 |
|---|---|
| Single Payment | 单笔付款 |
| Future-dated Payment | 未来日期付款 |
| Standing Order | 定期付款 / 常设付款指令 |
| Standing Order Revocation | 撤销 Standing Order |
| Future-dated Payment Revocation | 撤销未来日期付款 |
| Multiple Beneficiaries | 多收款人付款 |
| Non-saved Beneficiary | 向未保存收款人付款 |
| Payment Status | 从发起到执行 / 拒绝的状态跟踪 |
| Error Reason | 拒绝原因、限额、欺诈、收款人验证失败等 |

你们投标中可以把 PIS 做成一个独立升级包：

## PIS Expansion Pack

| 子模块 | 内容 |
|---|---|
| Payment API 扩展 | 单笔、未来日期、Standing Order、多收款人 |
| Revocation API | 撤销未来付款和 Standing Order |
| Status API | 查询支付执行状态 |
| SCA Dynamic Linking | 金额、收款人、账户绑定认证 |
| VoP 集成 | 支付前收款人验证 |
| Exception Handling | 拒绝、挂起、欺诈、限额、验证失败 |
| Evidence Store | 保存用户授权、SCA、VoP、支付状态证据 |

---

# 9. Verification of Payee：收款人验证

PSD3 / PSR 强化了 **Verification of Payee，VoP，收款人验证**。

核心目标是：

> 在付款授权前，检查收款人名称与账户标识是否匹配，降低转错账和诈骗风险。

典型返回结果可能包括：

| 返回结果 | 含义 |
|---|---|
| Match | 名称和账户匹配 |
| Close Match | 部分匹配，可能有轻微差异 |
| No Match | 不匹配 |
| Unavailable | 暂无法验证 |
| Error | 验证失败 |

对项目的影响：

| 模块 | 改造点 |
|---|---|
| Payment Journey | 在用户授权前插入 VoP 检查 |
| UX | 清晰展示 Match / No Match / Close Match |
| PISP Flow | PISP 发起支付时也要协调 VoP |
| Liability | 用户被提示后是否继续付款，需要留证 |
| Evidence | 保存 VoP 请求、响应、展示内容、用户选择 |
| Corporate Payment | 企业收款人可能涉及 LEI、EUID、税号等标识 |

VoP 不只是技术接口，它直接影响用户体验、责任分配和赔付判断。

---

# 10. Fraud、Liability 与 Refund：欺诈和赔付机制增强

PSD2 的核心是 SCA 和未授权支付责任。PSD3 / PSR 的方向更进一步：

- 不仅要看是否做了 SCA；
- 还要看 PSP 是否做了有效交易监控；
- 是否及时发现欺诈；
- 是否正确处理冒充诈骗；
- 是否有清晰的退款和拒付流程；
- 是否能证明用户是否 Gross Negligence；
- 是否能证明 PSP 自己已经履行监控和提醒义务。

## 10.1 交易监控

需要覆盖：

| 场景 | 监控内容 |
|---|---|
| Payer PSP | 付款前风险评分 |
| Payee PSP | 收款前或资金可用前风险识别 |
| PIS Payment | PISP 发起交易也纳入风险评分 |
| Device Risk | 设备、IP、地理位置、行为模式 |
| Beneficiary Risk | 新收款人、高风险账户、异常收款人 |
| Transaction Risk | 金额、频率、时间、账户历史 |
| Fraud Pattern | APP Fraud、Impersonation Fraud、异常转账 |

## 10.2 冒充欺诈

PSD3 / PSR 特别关注一种场景：

> 消费者被第三方欺骗，对方假冒银行、支付机构或其沟通渠道，导致消费者自己授权了一笔欺诈交易。

这种交易在 PSD2 下常常很难处理，因为技术上它是“用户授权”的。但 PSD3 / PSR 对这种场景提出更强消费者保护。

项目上需要设计：

| 模块 | 内容 |
|---|---|
| Fraud Case Management | 欺诈案件登记、调查、决策 |
| Police Report Capture | 用户报警证明记录 |
| Refund Workflow | 退款判断、审批、执行 |
| Evidence Review | SCA、VoP、风险评分、用户提醒 |
| Gross Negligence Assessment | 是否存在用户重大过失 |
| PSP Burden of Proof | PSP 需要证明自己没有责任或用户存在重大过失 |
| SLA Tracking | 调查和退款时限管理 |

这部分很适合作为高价值咨询 + 系统实施范围。

---

# 11. 用户限额、阻断和安全控制

PSD3 / PSR 对支付限额和支付工具阻断也更明确。

需要支持：

| 能力 | 内容 |
|---|---|
| 用户自设限额 | 按单笔、日、周、月或支付工具设置 |
| Remote Limit Increase | 远程提高限额可设置冷静期 |
| Limit Exceeded Refusal | 超过限额自动拒绝 |
| Instrument Blocking | 可因安全、欺诈、信用风险阻断工具 |
| Customer Notification | 阻断、拒绝、恢复都要通知 |
| Review Workflow | 阻断后需要定期评估是否继续合理 |
| Cross-channel Consistency | 网银、App、API、客服后台限额一致 |

这部分需要接入：

- Core Banking；
- Payment Engine；
- Fraud Engine；
- Customer Notification；
- Mobile App；
- Open Banking API；
- Customer Service Tooling。

# 12. 你们 PSD2 平台哪些能力可以复用？

这是你们投标时非常重要的商业信息。不要让客户认为所有东西都要重建。

| PSD2 现有能力 | PSD3 / PSR 下可复用部分 | 需要升级部分 |
|---|---|---|
| API Gateway | 路由、mTLS、认证、流量控制 | API 性能报告、错误码、监管证据 |
| Consent Engine | Consent 创建、OAuth 流程 | Consent Dashboard、撤销、访问日期、TPP 事件同步 |
| TPP Onboarding | 证书校验、目录查询 | 去除不必要注册，避免构成 obstacle |
| SCA Integration | 用户认证编排 | SCA Parity、AIS 180 天逻辑、非智能手机支持 |
| Account API | 账户、余额、交易 | Account Holder Name、Pending Transactions、渠道能力等价 |
| Payment API | 单笔付款 | Standing Order、Future-dated、多收款人、撤销 |
| Monitoring | 技术监控 | 季度监管报告、TPP 成功率、接口对比 |
| Fraud Engine | 风险评分 | Payer / Payee PSP 双侧监控、冒充欺诈、赔付证据 |
| Audit Log | 技术日志 | Article-level Evidence Pack、监管审计证据 |

你们的卖点应该是：

> **我们不是从零开始，而是在已有 PSD2 Open Banking 平台上做 PSD3 / PSR Regulatory Uplift。**

## PSD3 and PSR in one sentence

**PSD3** and **PSR** are the EU’s new payment-services reform package to replace and upgrade the PSD2 framework. **PSD3 is mainly about licensing and supervision of payment institutions**, while **PSR is mainly about the operating rules for payments, open banking, SCA, fraud, transparency, and customer rights**.

The European Commission published the package on **28 June 2023**: one proposal for a new **Payment Services and Electronic Money Services Directive, PSD3**, and one proposal for a **Payment Services Regulation, PSR**. The package aims to modernise PSD2, improve consumer protection and competition, support secure data sharing/open banking, and respond to new fraud risks in digital payments. ([Finance](https://finance.ec.europa.eu/publications/financial-data-access-and-payments-package_en))

---

# 1. What is PSD3?

**PSD3** means **Third Payment Services Directive**.

It is the successor to **PSD2**, but it does not simply copy PSD2. PSD3 mainly deals with the **institutional and regulatory framework** for payment service providers.

In simple terms, PSD3 answers questions like:

| Question | PSD3 focus |
|---|---|
| Who can become a payment institution? | Authorisation / licensing |
| What information must a payment institution submit to the regulator? | Application requirements |
| How should payment institutions be supervised? | Prudential supervision |
| What capital and safeguarding rules apply? | Own funds, safeguarding, governance |
| How are e-money institutions treated? | Integration of e-money institutions into the payment institution framework |
| What happens to existing PSD2 licences? | Transitional rules |

PSD3 is a **Directive**. Under EU law, a directive sets a goal for EU countries, but each country must create or amend its own national law to achieve that goal. That means PSD3 will still need national transposition in each EU Member State. ([European Union](https://european-union.europa.eu/institutions-law-budget/law/types-legislation_en))

For your project context: **PSD3 matters more for licensing, authorisation, governance, safeguarding, regulatory submissions, and payment/e-money institution status.**

---

# 2. What is PSR?

**PSR** means **Payment Services Regulation**.

PSR is the more important text for **day-to-day payment operations and Open Banking implementation**.

It covers practical operating rules such as:

| Area | PSR focus |
|---|---|
| Open Banking | AISP / PISP access to payment accounts |
| Dedicated interface | API access, availability, documentation, testing |
| SCA | Strong Customer Authentication rules |
| Fraud prevention | Transaction monitoring, impersonation fraud, liability |
| Verification of Payee | Checking payee name and account identifier |
| Customer rights | Refunds, transparency, information duties |
| Payment execution | Rights and obligations between users and PSPs |
| Fees and transparency | Clear information before payment |
| Cash access | Retail cash withdrawal and ATM-related transparency |

PSR is a **Regulation**. Under EU law, a regulation is binding and must be applied in its entirety across the EU. That means PSR is intended to create more uniform rules across Member States than PSD2 did. ([European Union](https://european-union.europa.eu/institutions-law-budget/law/types-legislation_en))

For your project context: **PSR matters most for Open Banking APIs, consent dashboard, SCA journeys, PIS/AIS rules, fraud monitoring, Verification of Payee, refunds, and customer experience.**

---

# 3. Why did the EU create both PSD3 and PSR?

Under PSD2, many rules were in a Directive, then interpreted through national laws, EBA RTS, guidelines, and local regulatory practice. This created inconsistent implementation across countries and banks.

The EU now wants a clearer split:

| New instrument | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **PSD3** | Who is allowed to provide payment/e-money services, and how they are supervised |
| **PSR** | How payment services must be provided in practice |

The European Parliament describes the overall package as targeting payment fraud, improving consumer rights, levelling the playing field between banks and non-banks, improving open banking, improving cash availability, and strengthening harmonisation and enforcement. ([European Parliament](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/legislative-train/theme-an-economy-that-works-for-people/file-revision-of-eu-rules-on-payment-services))

---

# 4. PSD2 vs PSD3 / PSR

| Topic | PSD2 | PSD3 / PSR |
|---|---|---|
| Legal structure | One main Directive, plus RTS and national laws | Split into PSD3 Directive + PSR Regulation |
| Open Banking | Introduced AISP/PISP access rights | Makes Open Banking rules clearer and more operational |
| API access | Required access, but implementation varied | Stronger requirements on dedicated interfaces, obstacles, dashboards and access quality |
| SCA | Introduced Strong Customer Authentication | Refines SCA and fraud-risk handling |
| Fraud | Focused heavily on unauthorised transactions and SCA | Adds stronger fraud prevention, monitoring and liability rules |
| E-money | Separate Electronic Money Directive existed | PSD3 brings e-money institutions into the payment institution framework |
| Harmonisation | National differences remained | PSR gives more directly uniform EU-wide operating rules |

PSD3 is expected to repeal PSD2 and the second Electronic Money Directive, while updating and clarifying the rules for payment institutions and integrating e-money institutions as a sub-category of payment institutions. ([European Parliament](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/legislative-train/theme-an-economy-that-works-for-people/file-payment-services-and-electronic-money-services-%28directive%29))

# 5. The easiest way to understand it

Think of the new framework like this:

## PSD3 = “Licence and supervision rulebook”

It tells regulators and payment institutions:

> Who are you?  
> Are you authorised?  
> Do you have enough capital?  
> Are customer funds protected?  
> Are your governance, risk and compliance controls acceptable?

## PSR = “Operating rulebook”

It tells banks, payment institutions, AISPs, PISPs and other PSPs:

> How must you process payments?  
> How must you provide Open Banking access?  
> How must you handle SCA?  
> How must you prevent fraud?  
> What must you tell customers?  
> When must you refund customers?  
> What API and consent-management obligations apply?

The PSD3 text says conduct rules are being moved into a Regulation, while authorisation and supervision of payment institutions remain in the Directive. # 4. Tower A - Bank / ASPSP implementation

This is the bank-side Open Banking implementation. The bank must expose payment-account access to AISPs and PISPs through a compliant dedicated interface.

## 4.1 ASPSP dedicated interface

The PSR final compromise text requires ASPSPs that offer online-accessible payment accounts to have at least one dedicated interface for data exchange with AISPs and PISPs. It also requires technical documentation, use of recognised standards, business-continuity access, and availability of documentation to authorised or applicant TPPs. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

Your ASPSP scope should include:

| Component | Deliverable |
|---|---|
| Dedicated API interface | Account, balance, transaction, payment initiation, status and consent-related APIs |

The PSR text also requires quarterly publication of availability, unplanned unavailability and performance statistics, plus a testing facility for authorised AISPs/PISPs and relevant applicant payment institutions. ## 4.2 API parity and payment functionality

The bank’s dedicated interface must not be weaker than the bank’s own online customer channel. The PIS functionality should not be scoped only as “single immediate payment.” The PSR text requires the dedicated interface to allow PISPs, at minimum, to place and revoke standing payment orders, initiate single payments, initiate and revoke future-dated payments, initiate payments to multiple beneficiaries, and initiate payments regardless of whether the payee is on the payer’s beneficiary list unless the user cannot do that in the customer interface. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

# 6. Tower C - PI / EMI migration

This tower is regulatory, operational and technical. PSD3 will be especially important here.

PSD3 lays down rules for access to the activity of providing payment services by payment institutions and for supervisory powers and tools. Undertakings intending to provide payment services must obtain authorisation from the home Member State competent authority, except where an exemption applies. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8222-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

## 6.1 PI/EMI migration scope

| Area | Deliverable |
|---|---|

| PSD3 authorisation gap | Gap against PSD3 Article 3 application information and Title II requirements |

| Governance review | Risk management, internal control, outsourcing, ICT, complaints, AML interface |

| Operating-model change | EMI becomes part of payment-institution framework while preserving e-money specificities |

PSD3 requires safeguarding for payment institutions providing relevant payment services or issuing e-money, including segregation or insurance/comparable guarantee options, and funds must be insulated in the interest of payment service users against claims of other creditors in insolvency. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8222-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

## 6.2 Transitional planning

For existing PSD2-authorised payment institutions, the PSD3 final compromise text allows them to continue providing authorised payment services without seeking new authorisation until the stated 27-month transition point, but they must submit information for competent authorities to assess compliance or decide whether authorisation should be withdrawn. Compliant institutions are deemed authorised under PSD3; non-compliant institutions risk suspension. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8222-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

For existing EMD2-authorised e-money institutions, the text similarly allows continuation during the transition period, requires submission of information for competent-authority assessment, and provides that compliant EMIs are deemed authorised as payment institutions under PSD3. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8222-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

|---|---|

| T0 + 3 months | Licence inventory, NCA engagement plan, evidence gap assessment |
| T0 + 6 months | Draft updated business plan, safeguarding model, own-funds model |
| T0 + 9 months | Governance, outsourcing, ICT, risk and control remediation |

Use relative dates until the final entry-into-force date is known.

# 7. Cross-cutting Tower - Fraud, VoP, refund and liability

This should be a separate workstream because it affects all three roles.

The Council’s announcement says the new rules aim to fight payment fraud, improve fee transparency and increase consumer protection. It also highlights spoofing fraud, fraud-information sharing between PSPs, IBAN/name matching before transfers, and PSP liability where preventive obligations are not fulfilled. ([Consilium](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2025/11/27/payment-services-council-and-parliament-agree-to-step-up-the-fight-against-fraud-and-increase-transparency/))

The PSR text places strong emphasis on transaction monitoring. It states that both payer PSPs and payee PSPs should have transaction monitoring mechanisms, including for transactions involving PISPs, and that payer-side monitoring should happen before execution while payee-side monitoring should happen before making funds available. Where monitoring obligations fail and fraud occurs, the payer should be refunded, with reimbursement consequences for the payee PSP where relevant. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

| Payee-risk monitoring | Yes, where payee PSP | Partial | Yes |

The PSR text also says that where the payer PSP has objectively justified reasons to suspect fraud, it must suspend execution; if it had such reasons and failed to suspend, the payer should not bear financial losses except where the payer acted fraudulently. The burden of proof that there was no breach lies with the PSP. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

For spoofing or impersonation fraud, the PSR recitals describe fraud where criminals pretend to be the consumer’s PSP and misuse PSP-attributed channels such as email address, telephone number, website or mobile application. The text supports a refund right for the full amount unless the payer acted fraudulently or with gross negligence. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8221-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

# 3. Step 3 - Change-control pool

## Evaluation

This is not optional. It is essential for your commercial protection.

For PSD3/PSR, change control is needed because:

1. PSD3 is a Directive and national transposition may create country-level work.
2. PSR is a Regulation, but follow-on EBA RTS/guidelines may affect details.
3. The April 2026 Council note refers to final compromise text with a view to agreement, not a simple already-completed implementation baseline.
4. The PSR compromise text explicitly assigns further work to EBA RTS for dedicated-interface availability/performance statistics and optimal recovery standards.
5. NCAs may interpret operational expectations differently.
6. The client may add countries, entities, products or channels during the project. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8220-2026-INIT/en/pdf))

---

## How to define the change-control pool

Do not call it “contingency.” Call it:

> **Regulatory and Scope Change-Control Pool**

or:

> **PSD3/PSR Regulatory Adaptation Reserve**

This sounds more professional and defensible.

---

## What should be chargeable through the pool

| Change type | Example |
|---|---|
| Final text delta | Official Journal text differs from final compromise assumption |
| EBA RTS/guideline update | New interface reporting, recovery-time or SCA technical detail |
| NCA-specific requirement | Local regulator asks for extra evidence or local process |
| Country expansion | Client adds another Member State |
| Entity expansion | Client adds another PI/EMI or banking entity |
| Product expansion | Client adds cards, wallets, merchant acquiring, corporate payments |
| API standard change | Client changes from custom API to Berlin Group/STET or vice versa |
| New VoP provider | Provider selected after design |
| New fraud scenario | Client wants extra fraud typology or case flow |
| Legacy-system discovery | Client discovers hidden dependency not disclosed in Phase 1 |

---

## What should not be charged through the pool

| Not chargeable | Why |
|---|---|
| Defects in agreed deliverables | Vendor responsibility |
| Rework caused by vendor misunderstanding | Vendor responsibility |
| Work already included in the SOW | Already priced |
| Normal project management | Base programme cost |
| Missed acceptance criteria | Delivery obligation |
| Poor estimation after confirmed scope | Vendor commercial risk |

This distinction is important. Otherwise the client may see the change pool as a hidden budget mechanism.

Create a simple Change Board.

# 1. First, fix the regulatory scope before cost estimation

Because you are a **UK bank**, be careful: **EU PSD3 / EU PSR does not automatically equal a UK-wide legal implementation.** For UK operations, the current UK framework still sits around the **Payment Services Regulations 2017** and **Electronic Money Regulations 2011**. The FCA’s Approach Document states that the PSRs 2017 govern authorisation and conduct rules for payment institutions, and that most PSPs must be authorised or registered under the PSRs 2017. It also says conduct requirements apply to PSPs, including ASPSPs, AISPs and PISPs, and that reporting/notification requirements apply to PSPs and e-money issuers. ([FCA](https://www.fca.org.uk/publication/finalised-guidance/payment-services-electronic-money-approach.pdf))

At the same time, UK Open Banking is still evolving. The FCA says it will consult on its proposed long-term regulatory framework for open banking before the end of 2026, and notes that UK open banking has approximately **17 million users**, nearly one in three UK adults. ([FCA](https://www.fca.org.uk/news/press-releases/fca-sets-out-vision-open-finance)) UK governance is also changing: the FCA has established a department incorporating FCA and Payment Systems Regulator capabilities, replacing JROC for open banking and open finance decision-making. ([GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/recommendations-on-payments-regulation-for-the-financial-conduct-authority-and-payment-systems-regulator-november-2024/joint-response-from-the-financial-conduct-authority-and-the-payment-systems-regulator))

For EU subsidiaries, EU branches, EU payment services, or group-wide EU strategic alignment, PSD3/PSR matters directly. The Council’s latest compromise text describes PSD3/PSR as a package to address PSD2 gaps, tackle fraud, increase consumer protection, boost transparency, improve access to cash, and adapt payment rules to new technology and market developments. ([Consilium Data](https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-8220-2026-INIT/en/pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

So your first cost-control move is to divide the scope into three buckets:

| Scope bucket | Meaning | Cost treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **UK mandatory** | UK PSRs 2017, EMRs 2011, UK Open Banking, FCA/UK supervisory expectations | Must be in baseline if relevant |
| **EU mandatory** | PSD3/PSR obligations for EU legal entities, EU branches, EU customers, EU-regulated payment/e-money business | Must be in baseline only for affected entities |
| **Group future-ready** | Features that align UK platforms to EU PSD3/PSR but are not yet UK legal requirements | Price as optional or future-ready package |

This prevents a vendor from making the project look bigger than it needs to be. It also prevents internal IT from under-scoping genuine EU exposure.
